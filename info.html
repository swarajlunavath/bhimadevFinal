    ECS - Electronic Clearing Service

    • Premium Payment through Electronic Clearing Service (ECS)
    Electronic Clearance Service of Reserve Bank of India is a well-established, quick, reliable, and economic processing for periodic bill payments. LIC offers this facility to its valued customers for payment of premiums. The service comes free of cost. Presently, this system of premium payment is provided at Mumbai, Chennai, Delhi, Kolkata, Bangalore, Kanpur, Vijaywada, Patna, Jaipur, Chandigarh, Thiruvananthapuram and Hyderabad cities only. The policyholders having accounts in the banks in the cites mentioned above are members of the clearing house, which are entitled to avail of this facility for all policies irrespective of the LIC branch to which these policies are attached for servicing.



    • Benefits to the policyholders:

    • Free service to LIC policyholders
    • Save precious time
    • Flexibility in premium payments.
    • No more hassles in sending premiums through postal, courier service, etc.
    • Fewer chances of the policy getting lapsed
    • Signing of a Mandate form, this mentions the date by which the payment will be done from your account.
    • This facility can be availed by the insured as well as the spouse and dependent children. It means the policyholder can claim I.T. relief for the payment of premiums.
    The procedure for availing of the service and the processes involved:
    • In order to avail of this service, the policyholders should have their Bank Account in an ECS Centre and the Bank should be member of the Clearing House.
    • Policyholder should fill up the ECS Mandate Form and get it verified and certified by the Bank where the Bank Account is maintained.
    • The Mandate Form should be sent to the nearest LIC Branch Office or to the Manager (CRM) of the Divisional Office with a photocopy or a cancelled leaf of the MICR cheque for further processing.
    • On the due date mentioned by the policyholder, LIC will debit the amount from the Account of the policyholder through the ECS process.
    • The policyholder should have sufficient balance to cover the premium due amount, otherwise it will be treated as a case of cheque dishonour and the policyholder would be informed accordingly.
    • The transaction process i.e. the first transaction after the authorisation takes around one month.
    • LIC will send the policyholders the official receipt by post after the premium amount from the respective bank account has been made. Policyholders willing to make payments through ECS process can do so by clicking on the link given below.
